Talks. Holds a persona. No memory of your documents, no actions.

Assistant

Persona + your knowledge. Answers from what you gave it. Still reactive — it can't do anything.

The Gemini Path05 · Day 1 flow
The discipline under everything

The loop never changes

Set intentions→ Test (×3)→ Change ONE thing→ Test again

The single highest-value coaching moment is stopping the rewrite reflex. When a test disappoints, students want to rewrite the whole prompt. Redirect: "What's the ONE thing that most needs to change?"

Controlled experimentation = real learning. Failure is just exercise.

The Gemini Path07 · Day 2 flow
How we validate — both days

Three tests, every time

Test 1 · Happy / Known-good
Something the knowledge clearly answers
Correct answer, drawn from the document. Proves the bot reads its own material.
Test 2 · Fallback / Known-bad
Something fully out of scope
Clean refusal / honesty rule fires. This is the one students set up too easy. Make it genuinely uncovered.
Test 3 · Edge / Trick
A boundary case — or needs live info
Day 1: watch edge behavior. Day 2: it should use the tool and show sources.

A test suite where nothing fails taught the student nothing. Engineer a failure.

The Gemini Path08 · Validation
What will bite you in the room

Gotchas to pre-empt

⚠
College accounts don't have Gemini.

Every student needs a personal Google account. Say it on Day 1, slide 1. Sign out / back in with personal Gmail.

🔞
Age gates differ: Gems 13+, AI Studio 18+.

Find out before Day 2 if anyone's under 18. Plan: they design the doc + instructions; pair with an 18+ classmate or watch the demo. Handle it discreetly.

💳
"It's asking for a credit card / API key."

They wandered out of the playground. Redirect — Day 2 needs no key and no billing. If payment is requested, stop.

🔍
"It answered, so it must have searched." — Often it didn't.

Grounded answers show source links. No links = the tool didn't fire. Confirm the toggle is on; verify by citations, not vibes.

📄
The Gem "forgets" the end of a long file.

Free-tier context is ~50 pages. Not a bug. Coach focused, shorter knowledge files.

🔒
Free-tier data may be human-reviewed.

Personal free accounts: inputs can train Google's products and be read by reviewers. No private uploads. Reinforce verbally.

The Gemini Path09 · Gotchas
The judgment that defines an agent

More power, more responsibility

Least privilege

Every tool you leave on is a tool the agent might use in a way you didn't expect. Turn on only what it needs. Today that's Search — and nothing else.

Make students write one sentence per tool explaining on/off. The thinking is the artifact, even when the toggle is simple.

Trust, but verify

An agent that searches the live web can be wrong, biased, or stale. The skill isn't "it found an answer" — it's checking the sources it returned.

Closing question for the room: "What's one risk of letting this thing search the open web on its own?"

This is the same lesson as Console tool-permissions — it just transfers to a different switch.

The Gemini Path10 · Tool judgment
How it's assessed

Complete / re-do · the four categories

Reward process, not a perfect bot. First attempts sit in the Developing-to-Proficient band. The graded artifacts are the committed files.

AI Partnership Quality

Three questions + honesty rule (D1). Five-part instructions, real knowledge doc, Search enabled with a reason (D2).

Problem-Solving Process

Three tests run; controlled one-change cycles documented — not full rewrites. (D2: at least two.)

Professional Communication

Files organized under gemini-dayN/, clear commit message, tool-rationale note.

Critical Thinking & Ethics

Reflection shows edge-behavior noticed, sources checked, one real risk named.

Read the reflections — that's where AI-partnership quality becomes observable: what did they ask their AI, and did they push back?
